Shadow Techniques Comparison

Projection Shadows

Description: Uses a projection matrix to project shadows onto flat surfaces without depth buffers or off-screen rendering.

Advantages

  • Simple Implementation: Easy setup with basic transformations.
  • Fast Performance: Fast, no additional rendering passes.
  • Memory Friendly: No extra storage for shadow maps.
  • Effective for Flat Surfaces: Works well on simple planar surfaces.

Disadvantages

  • Limited to Flat Surfaces: Cannot handle complex surfaces.
  • No Self-Shadowing: Reduces realism.
  • Static and Can’t Change: Fixed light sources, no dynamic support.
  • Unrealistic Geometry: Distorted shadows on curved surfaces.
  • No Blocking Shadows: Objects can't block shadows correctly.

Shadow Mapping

Description: Renders the scene from the light source’s perspective to create a shadow map, which determines shadow visibility.

Advantages

  • Works on All Surfaces: Supports flat, curved, and complex surfaces.
  • Self-Shadowing: Objects can shadow themselves for realism.
  • Dynamic: Handles moving lights and objects.
  • Realistic Blocking: Realistic blocking of shadows.
  • Versatile: Supports various light types (directional, point, spot).

Disadvantages

  • Aliasing Issues: Limited resolution causes jagged edges.
  • Shadow Acne: Precision errors create artifacts.
  • Bias Tuning: Needs careful depth bias adjustments.
  • Performance Overhead: Requires rendering the scene twice.
  • Complex Implementation: Requires depth buffers and shader logic.
